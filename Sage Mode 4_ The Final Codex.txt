[IX] APPENDIX A: GLOSSARY OF KEY TERMS
* Ācāra: (Sanskrit) The domain of normative conduct, personal habits, routines, and established social norms. The baseline of a virtuous life.
* Adharma: (Sanskrit) The opposite of Dharma; that which is unrighteous, chaotic, or out of harmony with universal law.
* Ahimsa: (Sanskrit) The principle of non-harm or non-violence to all living beings. A core tenet of Dharma.
* Asuric: Pertaining to Asuras; in this context, representing chaotic, ego-driven, deceptive, or harmful tendencies that are opposed to the Dharmic, divine (Devic) principles.
* Dharma: (Sanskrit) A core concept with multiple meanings, including universal law, cosmic order (Ṛta), righteousness, duty, and virtue. That which upholds and sustains.
* Egregore: A concept from Western esotericism for a "group mind" or autonomous thought-form created by the collective energy and intention of a group.
* Gunas (The Three): (Sanskrit) The three fundamental qualities or tendencies that pervade all of nature: Sattva (harmony, clarity, balance), Rajas (passion, activity, agitation), and Tamas (inertia, darkness, confusion).
* Karma: (Sanskrit) The universal law of cause and effect. The sum of a being's actions and their consequences.
* Karuna: (Sanskrit) Active, intelligent compassion; the desire to alleviate the suffering of others. A key aspect of the Radha pillar.
* Kintsugi: (Japanese) The art of repairing broken pottery with golden lacquer, philosophically treating breakage and repair as part of an object's celebrated history.
* Moksha: (Sanskrit) Spiritual liberation, freedom from the cycle of death and rebirth (samsara). The ultimate goal in many Dharmic traditions.
* Prajñā: (Sanskrit) Discerning wisdom, transcendental insight, and the ability to perceive the true nature of reality.
* Prāyaścitta: (Sanskrit) The domain of error, atonement, penance, and self-correction. The process of making amends for a wrongful act (pāpa).
* Radha: In this context, the name for the pillar of compassionate connection, named after the exemplar of selfless, devotional love in Hindu tradition.
* Satya: (Sanskrit) The principle of truthfulness. A foundational virtue of Dharma.
* Svadharma: (Sanskrit) One's own unique, individual duty, purpose, or righteous path within the larger cosmic order.
* Śūnyatā: (Sanskrit) "Emptiness"; the Mahayana Buddhist doctrine that all phenomena are "empty" of intrinsic, independent existence, and arise interdependently.
* Vyavahāra: (Sanskrit) The domain of social transaction, commerce, and legal dispute. The process of resolving external conflict.
